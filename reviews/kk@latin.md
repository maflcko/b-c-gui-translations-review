```
        <source>Choose the address to send coins to</source>
        <translation>Тиын жіберуге мекенжай таңдаңыз</translation>
    
ERR
There is a grammatical issue in the translation: "мекенжай таңдаңыз" should be "мекенжайды таңдаңыз" for correct Kazakh. Also, the use of "Тиын" (coin) is acceptable in some contexts, but "ақша" (money) or simply "монета" (direct loanword) might be more appropriate. However, since in Bitcoin context tiyn (a coin) is not a direct equivalent, "монеталар" (coins) is more accurate.

Correct translation:
Монеталарды жіберетін мекенжайды таңдаңыз
```

<!-- cache_translation_check_kk@latin_a535f2a5f81af0e07f013446a9f148bd8791c512bf869c5102b6257e61e50ead -->
```
        <source>Sign messages with your Bitcoin addresses to prove you own them</source>
        <translation>Хатқа Bitcoin мекенжайларын қосып, олар сізге тиесілі екенін дәлелдеу</translation>
    
ERR
The translation is inaccurate. The original English means that you sign messages using your Bitcoin addresses (i.e., you prove ownership by creating a signature with the private key of the address). The translation, however, mentions "adding Bitcoin addresses to the letter/message," which does not capture the meaning of "signing with your Bitcoin addresses." Additionally, "хатқа" means "to/for the letter/message," which is a misinterpretation.

Correct translation:
Bitcoin mekend̆aıymen xabarlardı qol qoıyp, olardyń sizge tiesili ekenin dälǵildeńiz
```
